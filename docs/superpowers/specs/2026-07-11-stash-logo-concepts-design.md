# Stash Logo Concepts — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-07-11  
**Status:** Approved concept set; awaiting final spec review

## Goal

Create three distinctive square app-icon concepts for Stash, a calm personal library that turns saved TikTok videos into durable music, coding, recipe, and inspiration cards. The identity should feel at home beside jazzy indie hip-hop, old-school hip-hop, coffee, new frameworks, and a developer's GitHub stash without imitating TikTok or GitHub branding.

## Shared identity

- The bookmark/stash silhouette is the dominant, immediately readable symbol.
- Music, coding, coffee, and source-control references are small secondary details.
- The mark must remain recognizable at small iOS icon sizes.
- The mood is tactile, warm, clever, and quietly technical rather than loud or corporate.
- Use flat, vector-friendly geometry with restrained print texture; no lettering, words, trademarks, mascots, mockup devices, or photorealism.
- Keep critical artwork inside generous safe margins and avoid transparent edges.
- Each final is a 1024 × 1024 PNG under 5 MB.

## Concepts

### 1. Crate Digger — primary recommendation

A bold bookmark whose inner grooves suggest both a vinyl record and branching code. Use warm cream, espresso brown, muted coral, and the existing Stash indigo. A tiny abstract commit-node detail may appear within the groove system. The result should evoke a well-loved record sleeve or independent coffee shop while staying clean enough for an app icon.

### 2. Coffee Commit

A bookmark whose negative space subtly forms a coffee cup. Two simple steam strokes hint at code brackets or a branching graph; a small circular groove supplies the music cue. The shape should read as a bookmark first, with the cup and code discovered second. Use cream, coffee brown, muted teal, coral, and indigo.

### 3. Midnight Stash

Layered bookmark cards on a near-black midnight background, with restrained teal and coral offset-print accents. Sparse nodes and connecting strokes suggest commits or a framework dependency graph, while a single groove or waveform supplies the music reference. This is the most underground, old-school hip-hop, and technical direction.

## Evaluation

Each concept will be checked for exact dimensions, file size and format, bookmark-first readability, absence of unwanted text or brand marks, clean edges, and legibility in a small thumbnail. The three concepts should feel related but visibly different when viewed side by side.
